## DECISIONS NEEDED FROM YOU

These are blocking — the documents can't be locked until these are answered.

### 1. Funding lanes — where does each investor's money go?
You now have three distinct places an investor's money could land:
- **ProjectARC / Deuterium3D** — your company, your wages, your hires, your tools
- **Terminal Games** — Andrew's company, his wages, his game development
- **Shared fund** — joint projects, shared infrastructure, split by contribution

The other investor who wants to commit wants to choose *which lane* his money goes into. This means the pitch materials need a clear structure showing:
- What each lane funds
- What the return structure looks like for each (is it all ProjectARC revenue share? Does Terminal Games have its own deal?)
- Whether investing in the "shared" lane counts toward ProjectARC revenue share or a separate agreement

**This is the biggest open question.** It changes the deal structure table, the CORE-NARRATIVE, and the Playbook. Answer this before anything else.

### 2. Mom pitch — confirm scope
You said: small tier only ($1K–$5K), founding supporter or project sponsor, never wages. Confirm:
- Is she getting pitched in Vegas too, or separately?
- Does she know about the company at all yet?
- Any sensitivities to navigate (re: the divorce, re: dad also being pitched)?

### 3. Slide deck — yes or no?
Options:
- **Printed deck only** (what's currently planned) — he holds it, annotates it, it's tactile. Works well for a dinner conversation with one person. Less formal, more personal.
- **Slide deck on laptop/tablet** — more polished, can include video/demo. Risks feeling like a corporate presentation at a dinner table. Better for a group pitch or a follow-up meeting.
- **Both** — printed summary he keeps, tablet for showing demos or video if he asks to see something.

My recommendation: **printed deck + tablet on standby for demos only.** The dinner is a conversation, not a presentation. But you know him better than I do.

### 4. The "honest sales" angle
You mentioned wanting to bring up how your current company does things dishonestly and how you want to do it differently — never lie, proper closing structure, keep things smooth. This is a strong character beat if placed correctly. Two options:
- **Work it into the pitch naturally** — during the "why back this founder" section, as proof of values
- **Save it for the probe phase** — if he asks "why should I trust you" or "what makes you different," it's a powerful answer that comes from real experience

Tell me which, or if you want both — and whether there are specific practices at your current company you'd reference (without naming the company in the pitch).
