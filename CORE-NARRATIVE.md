## What ProjectARC Is

ProjectARC is an AI systems company building the infrastructure that replaces entire production departments — not augments them, not assists them, replaces them — at a fraction of the cost and at quality that meets or exceeds what a human team produces in a realistic production timeline.

ProjectARC is the legal entity — currently a sole proprietorship registered under a GST number, with incorporation planned as the company scales. It operates two departments under one roof:

**Deuterium3D** is the public-facing brand: a film and AI technology studio. It handles cinematic production, short-form content, AI workflow tooling, social media strategy, and the OCC product. Deuterium3D is not just a creative studio — it is the live proof-of-concept environment where ProjectARC's AI systems are built, tested, and validated under real competitive production conditions.

**The Technology Department** (future incorporation as a separate entity) will develop and sell consumer and enterprise AI applications built on the systems developed through Deuterium3D. This department is funded by and grows alongside Deuterium3D, separating into its own incorporated company when the operation reaches the scale that demands it — a 3 to 5 year milestone.

Revenue generated by the Technology Department feeds back into Deuterium3D operations and shared infrastructure. The two departments are the same business at different stages of maturity.

**Investing in ProjectARC is investing in both departments.** The public face is Deuterium3D. The full business is ProjectARC.

## The Real Story — What Is Actually Being Built

Most AI companies are building tools that help people work faster. ProjectARC is building systems that eliminate the need for entire departments.

**The coding department has already been replaced.**

ProjectARC operates with a fully AI-managed development pipeline. Custom AI coding tools — built in-house — are in active use right now: building the Ringo video game, building the studio's film content, and building the next generation of tools that were not possible to create before these tools existed. Each tool accelerates the development of the next. The system compounds. This is not a future goal — it is the current operating reality.

**The modeling and texturing department is next — and it is 90% complete.**

ProjectARC has developed a two-tier AI asset pipeline that produces 3D models and textures with minimal human interaction:

- **Tier 1 (operational now):** A 2–4 image input pipeline producing near-AA quality assets suitable for mid-range and background use. Functional, fast, and already in active production use.
- **Tier 2 (in development):** A 20-image input pipeline with a custom reassembly algorithm that produces fully autonomous, movie-grade assets suitable for close-up shots at photorealistic quality — asymmetrical design, better output than what a human team produces in a realistic production timeline. The complexity increase is approximately 800% over Tier 1 inputs, but the result is fully autonomous production at a quality level that does not currently exist in a publicly documented pipeline.

The remaining development gap is a quality threshold, not a capability gap. The architecture exists. The pipeline is built. Tier 2 closes the loop.

**The orchestration layer — HKB — manages all of this.**

Both pipelines, the coding workflows, and all AI tool usage are coordinated by HKB, ProjectARC's proprietary agentic AI orchestration system. HKB is currently 75% complete. It is the core of OCC (the first sellable product) and the infrastructure backbone of the entire company. It is not a wrapper around existing tools — it is a proprietary system with architecture that, to the best of the company's knowledge, has no publicly documented equivalent.

**Deuterium3D exists to prove the tools work.**

Every project Deuterium3D produces — TRC, Ringo, the studio's social content — is a live demonstration that the systems perform under real conditions. The studio is not a separate business that happens to share a company with the tech department. It is the validation environment. When Deuterium3D wins a competition, ships a game, or lands a grant, it is proof that the AI infrastructure works.

## The Business Model

ProjectARC generates revenue across multiple streams designed to come online in sequence, each one reducing risk and increasing capacity for the next.

**Stream 1 — Terminal Games Partnership Revenue**
Deuterium3D is the contracted cinematic and AI technology partner for the Ringo video game. The studio receives a percentage of Ringo game sales, capped at approximately $100,000. This agreement is being formalized in writing. This stream is tied to the game's commercial release — it is the long-term return on the creative work being done now.

**Stream 2 — OCC (Orchestration and Creation Core)**
OCC is an agentic AI orchestrator that gives creators unified access to all major AI platforms — image generation, 3D modelling, code, writing, video — through a single intelligent interface. It knows the best use case for every AI and every model, routes tasks automatically, and manages API budgets across platforms. For heavy AI users it is estimated to reduce operational AI costs by 20 to 40 percent while increasing output quality and speed.

OCC is built on HKB. It is a significant expansion of HKB's scope into a consumer and professional product.

Target market, Phase 1: Power users who already understand API keys, use multiple AI platforms, and want to stop copy-pasting between tools and start automating. Niche but high-value with immediate willingness to pay.

Target market, Phase 2: Mainstream creators and professionals who want the capability of advanced AI without the technical barrier. Different product presentation, different marketing, same underlying system.

Pricing model: Monthly subscription, starting at $20 to $40 per user with user-provided API keys. Comparable tools in adjacent categories command $30 to $100/month. OCC's value proposition — unified access, automated routing, API cost reduction — justifies premium positioning within this range. Scales toward a managed subscription model as the user base grows.

After OCC is live, ProjectARC migrates its own AI usage to OCC-managed API calls — reducing internal costs while demonstrating the product from the inside. The studio is already operating at a level of AI cost efficiency that most comparable operations have not reached. OCC makes this systematic and sellable.

By months 4 through 6 of the initial funding window, OCC will be autonomously generating and launching additional vertical applications — tools for tradespeople, business owners, local companies, entrepreneurs, and creatives — adding new revenue streams with minimal additional development time. OCC builds the apps while development continues on everything else.

**Stream 3 — Consumer and Enterprise AI Applications**
Using the systems and workflows developed through OCC, the Technology Department will build vertical AI applications for specific industries. These products are post-OCC. They represent the technology department's long-term revenue and the reason ProjectARC will eventually split into two incorporated entities.

**Stream 4 — Social Media and Content Revenue**
Deuterium3D publishes development logs, AI workflow explainers, and entertainment content across YouTube and major social platforms. Content strategy is built around genuine interest — what is being made, what is being learned, what is worth sharing. It is not a marketing channel disguised as content. It is real content that also markets the studio and its tools.

Strategic alignment: Content will actively advocate for Unreal Engine 5, position Deuterium3D as a visible and contributing member of the UE5 development community, and directly support the Epic MegaGrants application by demonstrating established presence. This is a deliberate relationship-building strategy with Epic.

## The Game — Ringo

Ringo is a movement-based, story-driven first-person action game for PC and all consoles, developed by Terminal Games with Deuterium3D handling all cinematic and video content.

**The design philosophy:** The studio is building the game the market has been demanding and not receiving. Players are tired of predatory monetization, hollow trend-chasing, and games designed around extraction rather than experience. Ringo is a game you buy and own. The business model does not depend on player spending after purchase — it depends on building something people love enough to tell others about. The profitability of the company comes through AI tool sales, not in-game purchases. This builds a more loyal, more durable audience — the kind that funds sequels, adaptations, and everything that follows.

**Current development status:**

First conceptual playtest completed with 5 external testers. Results were significantly positive:

- One tester played continuously for 3 hours in an unfinished greybox state — until physical discomfort stopped him — because the movement system was that engaging.
- That same tester developed unintended emergent movement mechanics through extended play. The team identified these, documented them, and built adaptive optimization controls so emergent mechanics can be managed rather than patched out — accommodating both the specialized movement player and the general player simultaneously without sacrificing either experience.
- 4 of 5 playtest participants said unprompted: "I've never seen anything like this" or equivalent.
- The playtest revealed that the core movement system required optimization for broader accessibility. This has been addressed architecturally. The game now supports simultaneous optimization for both player types.

The playtest was run this early specifically to surface these issues before they became expensive to fix.

**Game roadmap:** 4-stage parallel development roadmap exists. Currently transitioning from conceptual phase into a short 3-level working Alpha. End-of-year release is the target, contingent on quality reaching AA game standards and studio infrastructure reaching sustainable scale. The game will not release until both conditions are met.

After the Ringo game: Deuterium3D produces the Ringo movie or show. Already in the plan.

## The Deal Structure

Revenue share. Not a loan. Not equity. No fixed repayment obligation. If the company generates no revenue, the investor receives nothing and is owed nothing. The investor's return is tied entirely to the company's success.

|  | Tranche 1 | Tranche 2 |
|---|---|---|
| **Amount** | $30,000 | $200,000 |
| **Revenue share** | 4% ProjectARC gross | 8% ProjectARC gross |
| **Return cap** | $60,000 (2×) | $400,000 (2×) |
| **Term limit** | 5 years | 7 years |

If Tranche 2 is invested, 8% replaces 4% — does not stack.

**Payment flexibility:** Lump sum or $5,000/month over 6 months at the investor's preference.

**Enforcement window:** The agreement includes a standard 6-month deferral on any wage garnishment enforcement. Capital deployed into an early-stage operation returns faster when it stays working inside the business during the growth window. This is a standard early-stage term, not an exception.

**Proprietary disclosure:** Certain technical systems and IP details are disclosed under NDA at the due diligence stage. This is standard practice for systems that have no publicly documented equivalent.
