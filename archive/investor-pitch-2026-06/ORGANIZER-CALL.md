# Organizer Call — VC World Summit Vancouver

**Number:** +1 646-535-1630
**Goal of the call:** confirm slot + format, and — the thing that actually decides the ticket — **who is in the Vancouver room as investors.**
**Frame:** you're an interested buyer doing due diligence on a $374 ticket, not a supplicant. They want your money; you're allowed to ask hard questions before you spend it.

---

## The one thing this call decides

You already know the prep is reusable no matter what. The **only** thing gated on this call is whether the ticket is worth $374. That comes down to one answer: **is there real density of active angels/VCs in the Vancouver room, of the patient-vision kind — or does it skew founders and service-providers?**

Honest read going in: pay-to-pitch "world summit" floors vary wildly, and the patient long-term angel you actually want is the type *least* likely to be working a transactional floor. So the burden is on them to show you the room is real. Vague answers = a no.

---

## Opener (warm, 15 seconds)

> "Hi — my name's Marek, I'm a founder looking at the Entrepreneur ticket for the Vancouver summit tomorrow. Before I buy in I've got a few quick questions about the format and who'll actually be in the room. Are you the right person for that, or is there someone who'd know the investor lineup?"

If they hand you off, repeat the line to the next person. Get to whoever knows the room.

---

## Questions — in priority order (ask 1 first, always)

**1. Who's in the room? (THE decision question)**
> "Who's attending Vancouver on the investor side — roughly how many active angels and VCs, what stages and sectors do they focus on, and can you name any confirmed funds or angels who'll be there?"

Listen for: real numbers, named funds/angels, early-stage / pre-seed focus, anyone tech- or creative-adjacent. Push gently if they're vague: *"Of those, how many are writing checks at pre-seed right now?"*

**2. Room composition**
> "What's the rough split in the room — investors versus founders versus service providers and sponsors?"

Listen for: investors a real share of the room, not a handful surrounded by founders pitching each other and consultants selling services.

**3. Slot + format**
> "Does the Entrepreneur ticket guarantee me a pitch slot? And what's the exact format and timing — is it the ~90-second elevator, is there an open-floor window, and when does that happen during the 1–5pm block?"

**4. After the event**
> "Do I get an attendee list or access to a contact platform afterward, so I can follow up with people I meet?"

(This matters more than the on-the-day pitch — the real value is the follow-up.)

**5. Refund / transfer**
> "What's the refund or transfer policy if something comes up?"

---

## Decision rubric (right after you hang up)

**GO** — buy the ticket — if **most** of these are true:
- They named real funds/angels, or gave credible investor numbers.
- Some of those investors write at pre-seed / early stage.
- Investors are a meaningful share of the room, not a token few.
- Slot + format confirmed; you get an attendee list afterward.

**SKIP the ticket, keep the prep** — if **any** of these:
- They can't or won't name who's actually attending.
- Answers are vague, scripted, or dodge the investor-density question.
- Room clearly skews founders + service-providers + sponsors.
- Evasive on refunds.

Either way you win: a go gets you reps and contacts; a skip saves $374 and the prep is yours for warm intros and the next room. The pitch is ready regardless — see `SUMMIT-PITCH.md`.
