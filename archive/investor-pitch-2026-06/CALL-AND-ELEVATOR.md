# Call + Elevator — VC World Summit Vancouver (June 18)

**Posture:** You're a founder with a working product, 1–2 months from a marketable launch, planning to attend. You're here to **fund this, learn, and build connections** — not to ask permission to be in the room. Confidence-first, no apology, never "here to learn" as a hedge.

---

## THE PHONE CALL (~1 min — logistics only)

> "Hi, this is Marek — I'm a founder planning to attend the Vancouver summit tomorrow. Quick few questions so I show up prepared. First, roughly how many investors will be in the room, and what stage do they typically invest at? Second, on format — I've got my pitch ready, do I get a slot beyond the quick elevator round, and is there an open networking window? And last — should I have slides ready, or is it more of a conversation floor?"

**If vague on investor count:** "Ballpark's fine — am I pitching to ten investors or fifty?"

**Four things you need off this call:**
1. **How many investors + what stage** → calibrate the pitch (early-stage/angel = your room)
2. **Slot beyond 60 sec?** → if yes, you'll improvise deeper based on the room
3. **Open networking window?** → the real value; where connections get made
4. **Slides or conversation floor?** → if slides matter, that's the one build for tonight

Then thank them and go. Don't oversell. The call is recon, not a pitch.

---

## THE 60-SECOND ELEVATOR (muscle memory — also your phone answer if they ask)

> "I build AI systems that have the capacity to replace entire production departments — not tools that help a team work faster, systems that can do the department's job. I'm proving it on real projects right now: building a full game and an animated film on nothing but my own pipeline, no outsourcing. The studio's the proof; the tooling's the product.
>
> The first product, before we expand into further territory, is OCC — it sits on top of every major AI tool and runs them together: picks the right model for each job, automates the work between them, manages the cost. The AI market's a mess right now — everyone's juggling four or five tools that don't talk to each other. OCC makes them one system that's smarter than any of them alone. And once it's live, it builds the next products itself — one tool becomes a pipeline of them.
>
> It's already running, and it's already got backing behind it — capital in from someone who's seen the work, and signed revenue agreements tied to projects in motion. The specifics live under NDA — that's a sit-down, not a hallway conversation. I'm raising to go full-time and hit the launch that puts it on the map. One to two months from a marketable product."

**Delivery:**
- "Capacity to replace" — NOT "replace." It's empowerment, not a threat. Never drop the "capacity to."
- The backing line: "capital in from someone who's seen the work, and signed revenue agreements tied to projects in motion." Both TRUE (the $5K believer + the game revenue-share), neither number quoted. Conviction on the table, specifics gated. Don't over-share dollar figures unprompted.
- The NDA line is a *gate, not a dodge* — deeper proof = a signed sit-down. Reads as substance, and protects you from showing rough/unfinished work in a hallway (the 6-sec Ringo spot can't go public for ~26 days; don't promise a visual you can't show yet).
- "One to two months from a marketable product" is the closer — makes this a *now* bet, not a someday bet. End on it, then stop talking.

## ONE-LINER BANK (pull these out on the floor, in networking, whenever they fit)

Not a script — a stock of sharp lines to drop on the fly based on who you're reading.

**On the business model / heavy users:**
> "People subscribe monthly, or just pay a small commission on what they use — so it makes money whether someone's a steady user or a heavy one. And it keeps the company safe: a heavy user can't run up a cost that sinks us, because their usage is what they're paying for."

**On the founder (proof you can build):**
> "Five months, alone, around a full-time job — built and in playtesting. I don't wait for tools to exist, I build them."

**On autonomy / where OCC is headed (for technical people leaning in):**
> "It launches as an operator that handles real workflows, and it's built to climb toward full autonomy on its own as the underlying AI tools mature — that graduation is designed into it, not bolted on later."

**On OpenRouter / 'isn't this already done?':**
> "OpenRouter proved the market — billion-dollar valuation just being the pipe between models. They're plumbing for developers. We're the layer above that actually does the job and hands a finished result to someone who isn't a coder."

**On why orchestration beats single tools:**
> "One high-quality output might take twenty or fifty steps a human doesn't have time to run. OCC runs them. It uses the tools out there better than the people who made them do."

**On the vision (punch line):**
> "To be a billionaire you make millionaires — so I'm building the tool that makes the millionaires."

## GUARDRAILS (true + discreet — protects the Tripo marketing relationship)

- **Funding:** you have a **$5K believer-investment (no expected return)** and a **signed revenue-share on the game (pays when it sells)**. Frame as "backing / capital in / signed revenue agreements" — never quote the small numbers unprompted, never imply revenue is already collected. Gate specifics behind the NDA.
