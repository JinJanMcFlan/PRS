## How royalties work here (so the adaptation clause is clear)

A royalty = an ongoing % paid to whoever **owns** a property by whoever **uses** it. Direction matters: Andrew owns the Ringo IP, so by default a Ringo *movie* would owe **him** a royalty — not the other way around. That's why the adaptation grant must state the royalty-back explicitly. Your leverage for **0% or near-0%**: the original concept was overwhelmingly yours, and you're doing all the game's cinematics — that's the consideration that buys clean screen rights.

Keep the two companies' books **separate** (not a 50/50 split of everything — that chains your accounting together forever). Clean model: each keeps their own medium's money, no cross-royalties (or a token one each way).
